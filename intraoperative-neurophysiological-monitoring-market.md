<p><p>Intraoperative Neurophysiological Monitoring (IONM) is a critical practice to safeguard neural functions during surgical procedures. The market is primarily categorized into three types: EEG (Electroencephalography), which monitors brain activity for signs of ischemia or seizures; EMG (Electromyography), which assesses the electrical activity of muscles and nerves to prevent nerve damage; and Evoked Potentials (EP), which evaluate the electrical activity in response to stimulus, ensuring functionality of sensory pathways. Each plays a vital role in enhancing surgical safety and outcomes.</p></p>
