<p><p>The standby generator market can be divided into two main fuel types: natural gas and liquid propane. Natural gas generators are typically connected to a home's gas line, offering convenience and reduced fuel costs. Liquid propane generators, on the other hand, require fuel storage and provide flexibility in remote areas. Additionally, generators are classified as air-cooled or liquid-cooled. Air-cooled units are smaller, easier to install, and suitable for residential use, while liquid-cooled models are more powerful, ideal for larger homes and commercial applications.</p></p>
