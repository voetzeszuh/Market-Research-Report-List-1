<p><p>The antibiotics market is segmented into various types based on their chemical structure and action. Cephalosporins are broad-spectrum antibiotics effective against Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria. Penicillins target specific bacteria, often used for respiratory infections. Fluoroquinolones are synthetic antibiotics effective against a range of infections, while macrolides are used for respiratory and skin infections. Carbapenems have a wide spectrum, particularly against resistant bacteria. Aminoglycosides are effective against serious infections, and sulfonamides are used for urinary tract infections. Other types include less common antibiotics and combinations.</p></p>
