<p><p>Transfection technologies encompass various methods for delivering nucleic acids into cells, crucial for gene therapy and research. Lipofection involves lipid-based reagents to facilitate the transport of DNA or RNA across cell membranes. Electroporation uses electrical pulses to temporarily permeabilize cell membranes, enhancing nucleic acid uptake. Nucleofection combines electrical fields with specific protocols to achieve high efficiency in primary and hard-to-transfect cells. Other methods include viral transduction, microinjection, and polymer-based approaches, each offering unique advantages depending on the application.</p></p>
